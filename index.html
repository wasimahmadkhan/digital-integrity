The Reality of Our Connected World

We live in an era where our physical and digital lives are inseparable. With constant connectivity, the threats to our privacy, our data, and our core values have multiplied exponentially.

Practical Guide: Securing Your Devices

Device & App Security

  • Biometric Locks: Always use Fingerprint, FaceID, or a strong PIN. Never leave your phone unlocked unattended.
  • App Permissions: Don't give microphone, camera, or contact access to basic apps (like a flashlight or calculator app).
  • WhatsApp Security: Enable "Two-Step Verification" in WhatsApp settings today to prevent account hijacking.

Network & Account Security

  • Public Wi-Fi Danger: Never use free campus or cafe Wi-Fi for banking apps, emails, or entering sensitive passwords.
  • 2FA is Mandatory: Enable Two-Factor Authentication on all academic, social, and financial accounts.
  • Consistent Updates: Keep your OS and apps updated to patch known security vulnerabilities.

Practical Guide: Spotting & Avoiding Scams

Common Tactics

  • Malicious Links: Messages claiming "Your bank account is blocked" or "BISP Funds arrived" are phishing traps. Never click.
  • Fabricated Urgency: Scammers create panic (e.g., "Send money, your relative is in the hospital"). Hang up and verify directly.

Core Defenses

  • The OTP Rule: No official bank, FIA agency, or university will ever call and ask for your OTP (One Time Password).
  • AI Voice Cloning: Be aware that voices can now be faked. Establish a "safe word" with family for financial emergencies.

Reporting Cyber Crime in Pakistan

Under the Prevention of Electronic Crimes Act (PECA) 2016, the FIA Cyber Crime Wing (CCW) is the primary authority to combat online fraud, harassment, and blackmailing.

Step-by-Step Complaint Filing Process

  • Gather Evidence Compile all screenshots, malicious URLs, transaction details, contact numbers, and emails of the suspect before filing.
  • Choose a Reporting Method Select the most accessible method: Online portal, official email, phone call, or a walk-in visit to a center.
  • Fill in Complaint Details Provide accurate information: Full name, CNIC, address, contact number, occupation, and a detailed description of the incident.
  • Submit and Save Complaint ID Upon submission, you will receive an acknowledgment via SMS or email containing a unique reference number. Keep it safe.
  • Follow Up Call 1991 or reply to the email thread using your reference number to track the progress of your case.

Scope of Cybercrime & Investigation

Crimes You Can Report

  • Online fraud & financial scams
  • Hacking & identity theft
  • Online harassment & cyberbullying
  • Blackmail & non-consensual image sharing
  • Social media account hacking

What Happens After Filing?

  • Verification: Initial check takes 12–14 days.
  • Investigation: Authority has 90 days to trace suspects via ISPs and tech companies.
  • Action: If valid, an FIR is registered. Penalties under PECA range from up to PKR 1 Million fine to 3–10 years imprisonment.

The Side Effects of Artificial Intelligence

Deepfakes & Manipulation

Highly realistic, fabricated audio and video designed to deceive the public, clone voices, or defame individuals.

Misinformation

AI models can "hallucinate" facts, enabling the generation of completely fake news and academic citations at an industrial scale.

Echo Chambers

Social algorithms trap users in ideological bubbles, reinforcing bias, restricting diverse thought, and polarizing academic discourse.

Using AI Responsibly in Academia

  • A Co-Pilot, Not a Replacement: Use AI to brainstorm ideas and structure research, not to write your final assignments.
  • Always Verify Output: Cross-check all AI-generated facts and citations with trusted academic sources and journals.
  • Guard Institutional Data: Never paste sensitive university information, unpublished research, or personal data into public AI chatbots.

Curbing Controversial & Blasphemous Content

As educated individuals, we bear the primary responsibility of maintaining the sanctity of our digital spaces and upholding religious and social values.

  • The 10-Second Rule: Pause before reacting to or sharing anything emotionally triggering, politically charged, or religiously sensitive.
  • Verify the Source: Distinguish between verified news outlets and anonymous social media or WhatsApp forwards.
  • Break the Chain: Refuse to forward unverified or controversial materials. The spread stops at your screen.
  • Report, Don't Engage: Use platform tools to report malicious content.

A Final Thought
